A stronger portrait of the business

A safe place to stabilize, grow, and move forward.

Life can change in an instant. Recovery, illness, job loss, divorce, incarceration, aging alone, or unexpected hardship can leave anyone searching for stability.

House of Shammah helps residents and families answer one urgent question: will this place help me rebuild my life? At the same time, our structured model helps referral agencies, hospitals, probation and parole officers, grant reviewers, and community partners understand how the program supports positive outcomes.

Our values

Human life is our greatest investment.

Communities are strengthened when people are supported through life's most difficult seasons with dignity, compassion, and practical action.

At House of Shammah, we believe every individual possesses value, purpose, and the ability to move forward. Through accountability, encouragement, structured support, and meaningful community partnerships, we help residents build the stability and confidence needed to pursue greater independence.

Our vision

Peaceful communities where people can rebuild with purpose.

Our vision is to cultivate peaceful, supportive communities where individuals experiencing life's challenges have the opportunity to stabilize, develop practical skills, strengthen resilience, and transition toward independent living with confidence and purpose.

Our mission is to walk beside residents as they rebuild their lives through the AAS 5-Level Integration Readiness System, guided by NARR Standards 3.0, nursing best practices, and Rhode Island BHDDH principles.

HOS provides a structured, faith-centered environment, peer support, wellness coaching, and individualized guidance that empowers residents to move from stabilization toward lasting independence.

Built on best practices

Structured, ethical, and recovery-oriented support.

House of Shammah is committed to operating in alignment with the principles of NARR Standards 3.0 and Rhode Island BHDD expectations by promoting:

  • Resident rights and dignity
  • Ethical operations and transparency
  • Recovery-oriented support
  • Safe and healthy living environments
  • Peer engagement and accountability
  • Community integration
  • Continuous quality improvement
